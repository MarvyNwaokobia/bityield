# Week 1 Discovery Log
**Stacks Foundry: Validate | May 24, 2026**

## What I explored

The gap between BTC holder demand for yield and actual BTCFi adoption on Stacks. The question I was testing: is this a supply problem (yield doesn't exist) or a demand translation problem (yield exists but the wrong people can't access it)?

## What I found

The demand signal is real and documented. A GoMining survey of 700+ BTC holders (CoinDesk, October 2025) found that 77% have never tried a BTCFi platform, yet 73% want to earn yield on their BTC. 65% could not name a single BTCFi project. Only 8% actively use BTCFi for yield or lending.

The infrastructure signal is equally real. sBTC TVL sits at $545M with the deposit cap removed. Zest Protocol has $70M+ in deposits. Dual Stacking has deployed $100M+ paying up to 5% APY in BTC. The Q1 2026 BitcoinYield report tracked APYs of 0.36% to 5.60% across four live Stacks yield products and found that 99.5% of enrolled sBTC sits in boosted positions — the people who figure out the current onboarding go all in. The people who do not figure it out never start.

The friction signal is confirmed by the ecosystem itself. Leather wallet launched a dedicated Earn web app in May 2025 specifically because the gap existed. Their COO stated: "We have to find ways to make Bitcoin DeFi accessible, practical, and simple for everyday users." Stacks' own 2026 roadmap names Bitcoin Staking as the top-of-funnel product for retail holders — explicitly designed for people without DeFi expertise. The Bitcoin Staking whitepaper published May 13, 2026 removes the biggest trust objection — BTC stays on L1, no bridge risk, full self-custody.

## What changed in my thinking

I came in thinking the problem was awareness — that people just did not know yield existed on Stacks. The evidence sharpened that. The problem is not awareness at the macro level. The problem is that the moment a non-technical BTC holder tries to act on their interest, the first step breaks them. They hit sBTC mechanics, STX gas requirements, five protocol choices — and they stop. Even the best UX attempt in the ecosystem right now is still a protocol directory, not a guided first-deposit experience.

## What I am testing in Week 2

The wedge I am taking into Week 2 is finding the words that make a conservative Bitcoin holder see earning yield on their BTC as the obvious extension of already owning Bitcoin — because 73% already want it, and the one thing standing between that desire and action is a conversation nobody in this ecosystem has had with them yet.
