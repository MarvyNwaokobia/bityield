## Why now

Three things converged in the past six months that make this the right
moment to build the consumer layer.

First: the sBTC deposit cap was fully removed in 2025. There is no longer
an artificial ceiling on how much Bitcoin can flow into Stacks. The
infrastructure can handle any volume of new capital from day one.

Second: the Stacks 2026 roadmap explicitly targets retail onboarding.
The roadmap calls out fee abstraction using sBTC for gas with no STX
required, passkey and WebAuthn wallet creation, and Leather Wallet UX
improvements as active 2026 priorities. The protocol is dismantling the
technical barriers BitYield depends on being removed.

Third: the Bitcoin Staking whitepaper was published in May 2026, backed
by 500 million dollars already paid out in yield. This is the first
mechanism where BTC stays on L1 under the holder's own keys while
generating native Bitcoin yield. The publication validates the exact
market narrative BitYield is built around.

## Competitive landscape

Existing products and why they do not reach the target user:

| Product       | Who it serves              | Why it misses the target user                       |
|---------------|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Zest Protocol | Existing Stacks users      | Requires sBTC knowledge and DeFi interface literacy |
| Hermetica     | Sophisticated yield seekers| Structured products with complex onboarding         |
| Dual Stacking | STX holders                | Built for people already holding STX                |
| ALEX DEX      | Active DeFi traders        | Trading interface, not a yield dashboard            |
| Bitflow       | Liquidity providers        | LP mechanics, not designed for beginners            |

None of these products are wrong. They are correctly built for their
actual current users. The gap is that none of them are designed for
someone who has never heard of Stacks. BitYield is built for that person.

BitYield is not competing with Zest, Hermetica, or Dual Stacking.
It is a distribution layer that sits on top of them. When BitYield
converts a new user, their capital flows into the underlying protocols.
BitYield's success is directly additive to the entire Stacks DeFi ecosystem.

## The user psychology

The target user is not opposed to yield. They are opposed to perceived risk.
The distinction is critical for product design and messaging.

Saying earn yield on your crypto triggers a risk association.
Saying your Bitcoin earns while staying on Bitcoin is a different frame entirely.

The sBTC guarantee — that BTC stays on L1 under the user's own keys — is the
only honest, credible response to the retail user's core concern. No other
yield product on any other chain can make this claim truthfully.

This is why BitYield is specifically and only a Stacks product, not a generic
DeFi yield aggregator. The Stacks guarantee is the value proposition itself.
The interface is the delivery mechanism.
